Listening

Learn about Listening

“ When people talk, listen completely. Most people never listen.”
― Ernest Hemingway

Listen to others effectively is very important. Others can provide valuable feedback that can help you improve you or your product or service, and they can alert you to issues that may be holding you or your company back.

Levels of Listening


Listening Skills

Tracking

Tracking what customer is saying

Actively show that you’re tracking what your customer is saying. Using statements such as “I see,” “I understand,” etc. Following your customers statements with tracking statements lets them know you’re following what they’re saying.

Empathetic

You should be Empathetic & understanding

Demonstrate concern. Empathy is one of the top traits that customers want when speaking to a contact center agent. Being an effective listener also means you should be empathetic and understanding of their issue(s).

Paraphrase

Repeat and/or paraphrase

Repeat and/or paraphrase. Show customers that you care and are being an active listener by repeating and paraphrasing what they say, so they know you understand.

Arguing

Avoid arguing

Avoid arguing or becoming defense. Upset customers will naturally try and take it out on you. It’s important that you listen to their issues and refrain from arguing or becoming defense.

Positive

Use positive language

Use positive language. When dealing with customer issues, try to refrain from using negative language. For example, instead of saying “don’t hit the red button”, you would want to say “the green button is the best choice.” Positive language helps to take away the stress that comes with customer service calls.

Uninterrupted

Don’t interrupt your customers when they’re talking

Never interrupt. Don’t interrupt your customers when they’re talking unless you’re just acknowledging with tracking statements. But never cut them off or interrupt them as they’re voicing their concerns.

Distractions

Avoid distractions

Avoid distractions. In today’s world it can be easy to become distracted. However, when working to resolve a customer issue, you’ll want to avoid distractions at all costs.

Questions

Ask questions

Ask questions. Part of being an effective listener means asking questions so you can get to the bottom of the issue, and how you can help resolve it.

Trust

Build trust

Build trust. As you speak to customers make sure you use an empathetic and friendly tone. While doing this let customers know that you’re doing everything in your power to help them. They need to know that you are a champion here to defend them, not an obstacle in their way.

Personable

Be personable

Be personable. Try to relate to customers as you speak with them. This will show that you’re actively listening and help to make the experience better for them.



Empathy

Empathy Works

Customer needs are rapidly evolving, and so are their queries and complaints. Customers now prefer services that solve their queries with genuine care and interest. And good customer experiences are built with EMPATHY

Empathy phrases or empathy statements words that convey consideration, care, and patience. Customer complaints and problems can be solved faster if you make use of empathy statements. Empathy statements vary from one situation to the other.

When do Customers Need Empathy?

Angry Customers

Angry customers are in the highest emotional state where they are likely to disagree with almost anything. Hence, do not try to jump to a resolution. First, empathize. Use empathy phrases to first bring them down to an agreeable state of mind.

  • “That would be frustrating to me as well.”
  • "I know exactly how it feels like to have experienced that”
  • “I can understand how frustrating it is when…(situation)”

Impatient customers

Dealing with impatient customers is tough as they are very intuitive. They want you to take them right across to the solution without a detailed conversation. And therefore, you have to be quick but empathetic.

Use empathy to first assure them that their problem is going to be solved. They will now participate in details because they received an assurance. Now you can trace back to the root cause by asking them questions. But remember not to over-promise to momentarily pacify them. This is likely to break the customer relationship if you fail to deliver what’s been promised.

  • “If I’m getting it correctly, you are saying that…“
  • “I’ll get this definitely solved for you“
  • “I’ll take you through this as quickly as I can for you, [add name]”

Know-it-all customers

Be extra careful while attending the know-it-alls. They tend to show that they are more knowledgeable about your product- more than you. They have their research done and are now into proving it. You cannot rebuke it or act negatively to it but stay empathetic.

First, appreciate the efforts they have taken to do the research. This brings them to your side. Then, attend them in a manner that you are going to add on to what they already know.

  • “I can see that you are very knowledgeable about our product. I appreciate that. But at the same time would like to add something very important to it…”
  • “Let me see if I can offer you a free trial period if that sounds good to you?”
  • “As you must be knowing…”

Dissatisfied customers

Listen to the customer, then ask open-ended questions about his experience. Verify what you think the customer has told you, then take steps to resolve the situation. Ask if you can do anything additional for him, also exceeding his expectations.

  • “Hold on for a second while I check that for you.”
  • “I’ve escalated the issue to our experts. They are getting it fixed right away?”
  • “Is there anything that you’d like me to explain?”

How to Win Customers With Empathy Statements?

To-Do Always: Positive Empathy Statements To Remember

Let them vent it out first

You never know what other reasons have made the customer so stressful. So, when an angry customer turns up, our support experts hear them out first. They give them time to explain what they are feeling and what they expect.

Listening to their viewpoints enables you to put yourself into their shoes. While listening, figure out how you are to frame the solution. Actively respond with subtle replies like “uh-huh”, “okay” in between. This assures them that you are not lost but genuinely listening to them.

Understand priorities

As a customer service representative, it is your responsibility to empathize with your clients. One tip to do this is to understand their priorities. Each client has their own particular list of priorities, especially during an emergency.

It is essential that you make their priorities your own priorities and address them in the same exact order. This will reassure your client that you are paying enough attention to their needs and that you know and what they want and are eager to take care and help them. How you treat you client’s priorities will show how much you value your clients because as a customer service representative, their happiness and satisfaction is your goal

Include key questions on call

Based on what is working for us,* show empathy by asking the right questions. *Some of the key questions customer service teams should keep in mind when talking to a customer.

Moreover, take the time to listen and offer to help where you can. Do not just gloss over the questions in a bid to get it over with and tick the empathy box. The customer will see right through you if you do so.”

Maintain a Positive Attitude Throughout

Sales and support both demand a positive approach. So, after the customer finishes explaining begin on a positive note of assurance, like, “Okay, I’ll get that fixed…”. Using ‘I’ gives you ownership – it’s as much a problem for you as it is for them.

No matter how perfect you build your product or service, problems happen. But how positively you resolve makes the difference. Keep your tone polite and respectful. Apologizing if it’s your fault is also an act of positivity. But do politely explain the reason and move ahead to quickly get them the answers. If praises come your way, stop to acknowledge. And before you end, always appreciate them for their valuable time.

To-Do Never: Negative Empathy Statements To Avoid

Never over-promise

Over promising, as said earlier, momentarily pacifies the customer. It’s good to go that extra mile. But in a race to satisfy the customers, you are giving them false expectations. And you end up dissatisfying them even more. Be honest enough to tell them the positives as well as the negatives.

For sure you must go ahead to ask them what more they are expecting. But aim to deliver what’s right for your business and your credibility. As quoted, “It’s better to under-promise and over-deliver than to over-promise and not deliver”

Never assume

Never assume that you have already found the solution, and jump to conclusions. Too often it is seen that sales and support agents ask a few questions. And assuming that they have completely understood the matter, they swiftly head for conclusions.

This not only leaves the prospect unheard but also can provide them with the wrong solution. Maybe they wanted to ask for something different. This creates miscommunication that can build negative customer experience. It’s better if you repeat their words at the end in brief. This also assures the customer that they were being patiently heard.

Do not be robotic, be Understanding

Showing empathy to customers shouldn’t be a new concept that is being implemented into customer service now that there is so much instability with the economic climate, pandemic, etc. Having said that, it comes down to having a human conversation with the customer. You’d be shocked at how many customer service representatives sound robotic in their responses, and in situations where customers clearly are just looking for empathy, being robotic can lead to them taking their business elsewhere

Demonstrate that you understand where their feelings of frustration or whatever emotion they are conveying come from, and go the extra mile to show that you want to genuinely help brainstorm a solution. If your company’s policies prevent you from finding a solution, be transparent while also saying that you’ll reach out to somebody up the ladder to see if there is anything at all you can do. Even if a solution doesn’t come out of it, it will show that you care about trying to find a solution.

Avoid negatively charged words

The best way to show empathy is to be solution-oriented and stop at nothing until you’ve resolved a customer’s issue.

Avoid using negatively charged words like don’t, won’t, or can’t. If there is an issue at hand, it’s up to you as a business to find a way to solve it. The moment you tell a customer you can’t fix their issue, they’ll go to another service or product provider.

More than that, when you go the extra mile to resolve a customer’s issue, you show that you care and appreciate them.

Appreciation and gratitude can go a long way in showing empathy to the customer.

What are service solutions?

Customer service solutions are products or services that businesses use to gain a deeper understanding of their customers’ needs and expectations. They work to streamline and improve customer communications, therefore increasing customer satisfaction. Organizations that use them are more likely to increase brand loyalty and profitability.

Good customer service typically means providing timely, attentive, upbeat support to a customer. It involves making sure their needs are met in a manner that reflects positively on the company. Whether you’re a large or small business, it’s vital to make positive customer interactions a priority. It’s particularly important in a call center or contact center.
